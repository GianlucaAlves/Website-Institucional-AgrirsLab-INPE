For small producers, strawberry cultivation has long represented a highly profitable alternative, essential for sustaining families and maintaining their properties. As mentioned earlier, family farming has benefited the most from the growing popularity of the sweet treat.
Many producers within this type of agriculture are now not only balancing their finances but also making investments that once felt out of reach.
In Espírito Santo, for example, strawberry production had already shown an increase during 2024. Growth reached 5.4%, totaling 32,884 tons, generating about R$ 396.4 million in rural income.
The state's high productivity—over 110 tons per hectare—has gained even more visibility in the local production chain thanks to the “strawberry of love.” The Agriculture and Livestock Federation of Minas Gerais (Faemg) also highlighted positive impacts in the region. A producer of large strawberries who sold a 12-unit box for R$10 was able to increase the price to R$15.
Another producer, who delivers around 2,000 boxes per week to Ceasa in Belo Horizonte, raised the box price from R$12 to R$25–R$30.
