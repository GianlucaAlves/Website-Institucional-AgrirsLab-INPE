About

The AgriRS Lab is a laboratory linked to the Division of Earth Observation and Geoinformatics (DIOTG) of the National Institute for Space Research (INPE). We are a remote sensing laboratory focused on agriculture, studying and monitoring crops using satellite imagery and geospatial data. We also develop research in environmental and social areas, such as deforestation detection and land use and cover change. We aim to connect technology, science, and socio-environmental responsibility to generate knowledge and support decision-making.

Mission

Promote comprehensive training and capacity-building for students, offering conditions for knowledge, development, and dissemination of technologies in the field of Remote Sensing. Support undergraduate and postgraduate education, research, and outreach in Remote Sensing activities at local, regional, national, and international levels through the integration of human resources and infrastructure support.

Values

We aim to succeed in our activities and therefore seek to share the following values:

  • Ethics
  • Justice
  • Commitment
  • Technical and scientific competence
  • Social and environmental responsibility

Future Vision

We are inspired to successfully carry out all activities that represent permanent purposes for the team, serving as a foundation to be recognized as a reference in the field of Remote Sensing in research, education, and outreach.

Work Methodology

  • Develop by integrating people and skills;
  • Teach by practicing and/or producing;
  • Produce by teaching;
  • Value creativity and integration.
