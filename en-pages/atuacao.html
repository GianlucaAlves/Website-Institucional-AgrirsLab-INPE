Areas of activity

Agricultural Monitoring and Mapping

AgriRS performs monitoring and mapping of the main agricultural crops grown in Brazil using remote sensing data. Classifications are performed using preprocessed satellite images and classification algorithms aimed at identifying cultivated areas with different crops. This approach allows tracking the spatial dynamics of crops across harvest seasons, contributing to agricultural planning and public policies aimed at the sector.

  • Carry out monitoring and mapping of the main agricultural crops grown in Brazil.
    • Employs remote sensing and preprocessed satellite images.
    • Uses classification algorithms to identify different crops.

    Allows monitoring the spatial dynamics of crops across harvest seasons.

    • Helps with agricultural planning and the creation of public policies for the sector.
    • Contributes to more efficient and informed agricultural management.

    Environmental Impact Analysis and Deforestation Detection

    AgriRS applies remote sensing techniques to detect land use changes, such as deforestation, agricultural expansion over natural areas, and degradation of native vegetation. These analyses can support environmental enforcement actions and more sustainable management of natural resources. This contributes to biome preservation and the development of more sustainable agricultural practices.

    • Vegetation Degradation: Analysis of the loss of quality and health of native vegetation, even if it has not been completely removed.
    • Agricultural Expansion: Monitoring the advance of cultivated or pasture areas over natural ecosystems.
  • Provides data and evidence to support control, fines, and actions against environmental crimes.
  • Directly contributes to the protection of ecosystems and biodiversity.

    • Yield Estimates and Crop Loss Analysis

    Based on time series of satellite images and meteorological data, AgriRS develops methodologies to estimate crop yields and assess losses caused by extreme events, such as droughts and frosts. These analyses allow identification of areas at risk of crop failure and provide strategic information for planning agencies, technical assistance, and crop insurance.

  • Use of remote sensing data collected sequentially over time to monitor the vegetative development of crops.
  • Estimate Crop Productivity: Provide quantitative forecasts about expected crop yields.
  • Identify Risk Areas: Map regions with a high probability of crop failure before the damage becomes irreversible.
  • Analyze the impact of adverse climatic phenomena, such as droughts (prolonged dry periods) and frosts (sudden temperature drops).

    • Crop Phenology

    At AgriRS, monitoring crop phenology is carried out through time series of spectral indices derived from satellite images. This approach makes it possible to identify important stages of the crop cycle, such as planting dates, grain filling, flowering and harvest.

  • We monitor the life cycle of crops (phenology) using remote data. This allows tracking plant development without the need for frequent field inspections.
  • Use of mathematical calculations on the different color bands captured by satellites (such as NDVI – Normalized Difference Vegetation Index), which measure vegetation health and density.
  • Analysis of the variation of these indices over time (days, weeks or months). The development curve of the spectral index reflects the "signature" of crop growth, allowing identification of the stage the plant is in.
  • Planting dates, flowering, grain filling and harvest (maturation/senescence)
